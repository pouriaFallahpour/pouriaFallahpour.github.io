Since October 2024, I am a postdoctoral researcher in Quantum Information team of Sorbonne University working with Alex Grilo. Before that, I received my PhD in computer science from École Normale Supérieure de Lyon under the supervision of Damien Stehlé and Gilles Villard . I received my Master's degree in theoretical computer science from the same university.

I am interested in a broad range of topics in computer science and more specifically in cryptography. My research focus is on analyzing the security of cryptographic schemes such as encryptions, signatures, and zero-knowledge proofs in the presence of quantum computers. On a more foundational side, I am very interested in exploring the possibility of cryptography merely based on generic hardness assumptions such as P ≠ NP.
